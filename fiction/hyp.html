The Love Hypothesis

Author
Ali Hazelwood
Publisher
Penguin
Publishing date
2021

Summary

Olive Smith, a third-year Ph.D. candidate, does not believe in long-term romantic relationships; however, her best friend does, which is what landed her in this predicament. It was always going to take more than Jedi mind tactics to convince Anh that Olive is dating and well on her way to a happily ever after: scientists demand evidence. Olive panics and kisses the first male she sees, as any self-respecting biologist would.

This individual is none other than Adam Carlsen, a young professor and notorious jerk. Therefore, Olive is astonished when Stanford's reigning lab dictator agrees to keep her ruse a secret and be her fake fiancé. But when a major scientific conference goes awry, placing Olive's career in jeopardy, Adam surprises her once more with his unwavering support and even more unwavering...six-pack abs.

Suddenly, their experiment feels perilously close to igniting. And Olive discovers that the only thing more difficult than a love hypothesis is examining her own heart under a microscope.
